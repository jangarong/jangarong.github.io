## <b>Canada (Toronto and Ottawa)</b>

### nomnomnom Poutine
This is the most poutine-y poutine I've had this vacation!
The food truck was ran by a Quebecer, and had squeaky cheese, savoury gravy, and thin cut fries!
Honestly quite a rare find in all of Canada...

### Toronto Public Reference Library
My favourite Toronto landmark, if it even is one. Architecture inside is very futuristic-y,
was featured in Scott Pilgrim. Haven't been to any place similar to this building at all.

### Rideau Canal Skating
When the weather is consistently -20C, the canal opens for skating! It's the longest
natural skating rink spanning 8km. I was lucky enough to skate on it before leaving, since
in recent years, the Rideau Canal rarely opens.

## <b>Japan (Tokyo and Kyoto)</b>

### Akihabara
Lots of anime and manga, although my favourite parts of this place was the huge variety of
arcades available!

### Sushi Ichijo
Ate their sushi for lunch since it was relatively low cost, had open reservations, and
recently got a Michelin star, and boy did it have a very huge variety! From what I remember,
there's eel, shrimp, cuttlefish, blue tuna, pufferfish, and many, many more! Probably the
best sushi dining experience I've ever had, and arguably the best meal I had in Japan!

### Shikansen
Almost as fast as a plane, can pay $100 USD for reserved Green Car tickets (basically first
class), and they run every 15 minutes! No need to go through security gates, and the seats are
very, very comfortable :))
